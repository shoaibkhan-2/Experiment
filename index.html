Math Anxiety Scale (AMAS)

9 quick statements about how you feel in maths situations — lectures, exams, homework. Rate each on a 1–5 scale from Low Anxiety to High Anxiety. There are no right or wrong answers.

"Taking an exam in a maths course" → 1 2 3 4 5
"Starting a new chapter in a maths book" → 1 2 3 4 5
9 items total · takes about 90 seconds
3
~5 min

⚡ Task 1: Cued Task Switching

This runs fullscreen on a black background. Each trial: a fixation cross appears → a cue word tells you which rule to use → a coloured shape flashes on screen. You press A or L within 2 seconds.

COLOR cue → press A for Yellow · L for Blue
SHAPE cue → press A for Circle · L for Rectangle
10 practice trials first, then 50 real trials
Your results shown at the end of this task
4
~12 min

🧮 Task 2: Operation Span (OSPAN)

The most challenging part — but also the most interesting! You do two things at once: solve a maths problem, then see a letter to memorise. After each set, recall all the letters in order.

Step 1: See maths problem e.g. ( 6 × 2 ) + 3 = ?
Step 2: Is the answer TRUE or FALSE?
Step 3: Remember the letter shown e.g. F
Repeat 3–7 times, then recall all letters in order
3 practice phases first so you know exactly what to do
5
~2 min

State Anxiety Scale (STAI)

20 short statements about how you feel right now, in this moment. Rate each from Not at all to Very much so. Captures how your anxiety level may have shifted after the tasks.

"I feel calm" → Not at all / A little / Somewhat / Very much
"I am tense" → Not at all / A little / Somewhat / Very much
20 items · takes about 2 minutes

Can I do it on my phone?
The keyboard tasks need a laptop or desktop — a phone won't work for pressing A/L keys.
Can I pause halfway?
Yes — your progress is auto-saved in the browser. If you close and reopen the page, it will ask if you want to continue.
Will I see my results?
Yes! After each task you'll see your reaction times and scores. Real data, not just "thanks for participating."
Is my data safe?
All responses are anonymous. Your name is never stored online. Data goes directly to the researcher's encrypted Google Sheet.
